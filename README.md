# TRIBE SALES PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

## Description
This project analyses Tribeâ€™s sales transaction data to understand how revenue and sales volume vary across regions, markets, stores, and product categories. The goal is to generate practical insights that support revenue growth, market prioritisation, and sales strategy decisions.

## Objective
The objective of this project is to evaluate sales performance across regions, markets, and lines of business, and to identify the key drivers of revenue and units sold to support data-driven decision-making.

## Business Questions
The analysis was guided by the following questions:
1. How many units are sold across each region?
2. How does revenue relate to units sold?
3. How does revenue vary across different lines of business?
4. Which regions generate the highest total revenue?
5. How many units are sold within each line of business?
6. Which markets contribute the most to overall revenue?
7. How is revenue distributed across stores within each region?

## Dataset Overview

- **Total Columns:** 10  

## Key Columns
Region, Market, Store, Trade Date, Fiscal Period, Model, Line of Business, Day Category, Revenue, Units Sold

## Data Cleaning & Preparation
The dataset was cleaned to ensure accuracy, consistency, and reliability before analysis:
- Standardised categorical fields such as Region, Market, and Store
- Corrected inconsistent date formats in Trade Date and Fiscal Period
- Removed duplicate records were identified
- Validated numeric fields (Revenue and Units Sold)
- Handled missing and blank values appropriately

## Key Insights
- The North East region records the highest units sold and total revenue.
- Units sold show a strong positive relationship with revenue, indicating a volume-driven sales model.
- Service Plans generate the highest revenue, while Parts record the highest units sold.
- A small number of stores contribute a significant share of total regional revenue.
- Revenue is concentrated in a few major markets.
- Ekiti emerges as the highest revenue-generating market.

## Conclusion
Sales performance is strongly influenced by regional and market dynamics, with the North East leading in both revenue and sales volume. While Service Plans drive revenue and Parts drive volume, revenue concentration across specific markets and stores presents opportunities for targeted optimisation and strategic diversification.
